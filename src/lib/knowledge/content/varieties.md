# Brazilian Mango Varieties

## Tommy Atkins
Tommy Atkins is the most widely cultivated and exported mango variety in Brazil, accounting for approximately 80% of all Brazilian mango exports. It is known for its:
- **Appearance**: Oval shape, medium to large size (450-650g), red-orange skin with green and yellow undertones
- **Flesh**: Firm, fibrous texture with medium sweetness
- **Season**: September to January (peak October-November)
- **Shelf Life**: Excellent, up to 21 days refrigerated, making it ideal for export
- **Main Growing Regions**: Vale do São Francisco (Petrolina-Juazeiro), Northeast Brazil

## Palmer
Palmer is the second most exported variety, prized for its superior eating quality:
- **Appearance**: Elongated shape, large size (400-600g), purple-red skin
- **Flesh**: Smooth, virtually fiberless, sweet with rich flavor
- **Sugar Content**: 16-18° Brix (higher than Tommy Atkins)
- **Season**: October to February
- **Growing Regions**: Vale do São Francisco, Minas Gerais

## Haden
One of the oldest commercial varieties, parent of many modern cultivars:
- **Appearance**: Round to oval, medium size (350-450g), yellow-orange skin with red blush
- **Flesh**: Rich, sweet flavor with mild fiber
- **Historical Significance**: One of the first commercial mangos introduced to Brazil
- **Season**: November to January

## Kent
Premium variety valued for its exceptional flavor:
- **Appearance**: Large, oval shape (500-750g), greenish-yellow skin with red blush
- **Flesh**: Rich, sweet, juicy with minimal fiber
- **Sugar Content**: 17-20° Brix
- **Season**: December to February
- **Note**: Higher susceptibility to anthracnose, limiting export potential

## Keitt
Large, late-season variety:
- **Appearance**: Very large (600-900g), oval shape, stays green even when ripe
- **Flesh**: Sweet, juicy, limited fiber
- **Season**: January to March (latest season)
- **Advantage**: Extends Brazilian export season

## Rosa (or Rosinha)
Traditional Brazilian variety:
- **Appearance**: Small (200-300g), pink-red skin
- **Flesh**: Very sweet, aromatic
- **Use**: Primarily domestic market, juice production
- **Regions**: Traditional cultivation throughout Northeast

## Espada (Sword Mango)
Native Brazilian variety named for its elongated shape:
- **Appearance**: Long, narrow, sword-like shape (300-400g), green-yellow skin
- **Flesh**: Fibrous but highly aromatic
- **Use**: Fresh consumption, juices, traditional cooking
- **Cultural Significance**: Traditional variety dating to colonial period

## Ubá
Small variety used primarily for processing:
- **Appearance**: Small (120-180g), yellow-green skin
- **Flesh**: High fiber, very sweet and aromatic
- **Use**: Juice concentrate, pulp production, compotes
- **Main Region**: Minas Gerais (Zona da Mata)

## Carlota
Traditional aromatic variety:
- **Appearance**: Small to medium (200-350g), yellow skin
- **Flesh**: Extremely aromatic, sweet
- **Season**: November to January
- **Use**: Fresh market, artisanal products
