# Nutritional Information - Brazilian Mangos

## Basic Nutrition Facts (per 100g fresh mango)

### Macronutrients
- **Calories**: 60 kcal
- **Carbohydrates**: 15g
  - Sugars: 13.7g (fructose, glucose, sucrose)
  - Dietary Fiber: 1.6g
- **Protein**: 0.8g
- **Fat**: 0.4g
- **Water**: 83g

### Vitamins

#### Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)
- **Amount**: 36.4mg (40% Daily Value)
- **Benefits**: Immune support, collagen synthesis, antioxidant
- **Note**: Brazilian mangos are excellent source, higher than many tropical fruits

#### Vitamin A (as Beta-Carotene)
- **Amount**: 54µg RAE (6% DV)
- **Beta-Carotene**: 640µg
- **Benefits**: Eye health, immune function, skin health
- **Note**: Palmer variety contains highest levels

#### Folate (Vitamin B9)
- **Amount**: 43µg (11% DV)
- **Benefits**: Cell division, DNA synthesis, pregnancy support

#### Vitamin B6
- **Amount**: 0.12mg (7% DV)
- **Benefits**: Metabolism, brain function

#### Vitamin E
- **Amount**: 0.9mg (6% DV)
- **Benefits**: Antioxidant, skin health

#### Vitamin K
- **Amount**: 4.2µg (4% DV)
- **Benefits**: Blood clotting, bone health

### Minerals

#### Potassium
- **Amount**: 168mg (4% DV)
- **Benefits**: Blood pressure regulation, muscle function

#### Magnesium
- **Amount**: 10mg (2% DV)
- **Benefits**: Enzyme function, nerve transmission

#### Copper
- **Amount**: 0.11mg (12% DV)
- **Benefits**: Iron metabolism, antioxidant enzymes

## Antioxidant Compounds

### Polyphenols
- **Mangiferin**: Unique to mangos, powerful antioxidant
- **Quercetin**: Anti-inflammatory properties
- **Gallic Acid**: Free radical scavenger
- **Catechins**: Similar to those in green tea

### Carotenoids
- **Beta-Carotene**: Highest in ripe yellow-fleshed varieties
- **Luteoxanthin**: Eye health
- **Violaxanthin**: Antioxidant

## Health Benefits

### 1. Immune System Support
- High vitamin C content
- Vitamin A for mucosal immunity
- Antioxidant protection

### 2. Digestive Health
- Dietary fiber aids digestion
- Digestive enzymes (amylases) help break down carbs
- Prebiotic compounds support gut bacteria

### 3. Eye Health
- Beta-carotene converts to vitamin A
- Zeaxanthin and lutein protect retina
- May reduce macular degeneration risk

### 4. Skin Health
- Vitamin C for collagen production
- Vitamin A for cell turnover
- Polyphenols protect against UV damage

### 5. Heart Health
- Potassium supports healthy blood pressure
- Fiber helps manage cholesterol
- Antioxidants reduce oxidative stress

### 6. Blood Sugar Management
- Despite sweetness, has moderate glycemic index (GI: 51)
- Fiber slows sugar absorption
- Mangiferin may improve insulin sensitivity

## Variety-Specific Nutrition

### Tommy Atkins
- Moderate vitamin C: 28mg/100g
- Good fiber content
- Slightly lower sugar

### Palmer
- Higher beta-carotene (deeper orange flesh)
- Sweeter: 16-18° Brix
- Rich, creamy texture indicates higher fat-soluble vitamins

### Kent
- Highest sugar content: up to 20° Brix
- Rich in carotenoids
- Lower acidity

## Storage Impact on Nutrition

### Fresh (Peak Ripeness)
- Maximum vitamin C content
- Optimal enzyme activity
- Best flavor compounds

### Refrigerated (2-5°C)
- Vitamin C decreases 10-15% per week
- Carotenoids stable
- Texture may suffer (chilling injury below 10°C)

### Frozen
- Retains most nutrients
- Some texture loss
- Good for smoothies and processing

### Dried
- Concentrated sugars and calories
- Some vitamin C loss
- Fiber and minerals concentrated

## Recommended Intake
- **General**: 1 medium mango (200g) provides excellent daily nutrition
- **Vitamin C**: One mango provides 70-100% of daily needs
- **Calories**: Moderate consumption recommended for those watching sugar intake
