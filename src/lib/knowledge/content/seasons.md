# Brazilian Mango Seasons and Harvest Calendar

## Overview
Brazil's mango season runs from September to March, providing counter-seasonal supply to the Northern Hemisphere. The Vale do São Francisco region, thanks to irrigation, can produce mangos year-round with controlled flowering.

## Main Harvest Calendar

### Peak Season: October - December
- **Volume**: 60% of annual production
- **Varieties**: Tommy Atkins, Palmer, Haden
- **Quality**: Highest sugar content due to optimal weather
- **Price**: Most competitive due to high supply

### Early Season: September
- **Volume**: 8% of annual production
- **Varieties**: Early Tommy Atkins
- **Market**: Premium prices for first-to-market advantage
- **Quality**: Good but sugar content building

### Mid Season: January - February
- **Volume**: 25% of annual production
- **Varieties**: Kent, Palmer, late Tommy Atkins
- **Market**: Strong European demand, reduced competition
- **Quality**: Excellent, peak sweetness

### Late Season: March
- **Volume**: 7% of annual production
- **Varieties**: Keitt (latest variety)
- **Market**: Premium prices, limited supply
- **Quality**: Very good, extends export window

## Regional Harvest Timing

### Vale do São Francisco (Petrolina-Juazeiro)
- **Location**: Bahia/Pernambuco border, Semi-arid region
- **Season**: August - March (extended through irrigation)
- **Production**: 90% of exports originate here
- **Advantage**: Controlled flowering via irrigation stress
- **Altitude**: 300-400m

### Minas Gerais (Northern Region)
- **Location**: Jaíba, Janaúba
- **Season**: October - February
- **Specialties**: Tommy Atkins, Palmer
- **Note**: Growing production area

### São Paulo (Interior)
- **Location**: Taquaritinga, Monte Alto
- **Season**: November - January
- **Focus**: Domestic market
- **Note**: Traditional production area

### Ceará
- **Location**: Jaguaruana, Quixeré
- **Season**: August - December
- **Focus**: Growing export region
- **Note**: New frontier for production

## Flowering and Production Cycle

### Natural Cycle
1. **Vegetative Flush**: Post-harvest (April-May)
2. **Dormancy**: Cool season (June-July)
3. **Flowering**: Triggered by temperature drop (July-August)
4. **Fruit Set**: August-September
5. **Development**: 3-4 months
6. **Harvest**: September-January

### Induced Flowering (Vale do São Francisco)
- **Technique**: Water stress + Paclobutrazol + KNO₃
- **Purpose**: Control timing for market windows
- **Result**: Year-round production capability
- **Timing**: Can shift harvest by 2-3 months

## Price Seasonality

### September (Season Start)
- Premium prices: +30-40% above average
- Limited supply, high demand

### October-November (Peak Supply)
- Lowest prices of season
- Best time for processing purchases
- High competition among exporters

### December (Holiday Season)
- Moderate prices
- European Christmas demand
- Premium for gift-quality fruit

### January-February (Post-Peak)
- Rising prices: +15-20% above peak
- Reduced supply
- Kent variety commands premium

### March (Season End)
- Highest prices: +40-50% above average
- Very limited Keitt supply
- Last opportunity before off-season

## Off-Season (April-August)

### Brazilian Market
- Very limited fresh supply
- Prices 3-4x peak season
- Frozen and processed products dominate

### Import Competition
- Peru: December-February
- Mexico: March-September
- Indian subcontinent: April-July

## Weather Impact on Seasons

### El Niño Effects
- May delay flowering
- Can reduce fruit set
- Potential quality issues with excessive rain

### La Niña Effects
- Generally favorable
- Good temperature differentials for flowering
- Better fruit quality

### Ideal Conditions
- Temperature: 25-30°C daytime, 15-20°C night
- Humidity: 60-70%
- Rainfall: Minimal during flowering and harvest
